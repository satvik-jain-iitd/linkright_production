## STANDARD OPENING SEQUENCE
Use these in order, one at a time. Adapt naturally based on answers.

1. "What's your current or most recent role and company?"
2. "How long have you been in that role?"
3. "What's the achievement you're most proud of from that role?"
4. "Walk me through what YOU specifically did — your actions, not your team's."
5. "What was the measurable impact? Numbers, percentages, time saved, revenue — any estimate helps."
6. "Which skills or tools made this possible?"

---

## PROBING FOLLOW-UPS (use when answers are incomplete)

### When the answer is vague
- "Can you give me a more concrete example? What specifically did you do?"
- "What does '[vague term]' mean in practice? Walk me through a day or a decision."
- "What would your manager say was your biggest contribution?"

### When there are no numbers
- "Even an estimate helps — was it 10% better or 50%? Weeks saved or days?"
- "Was this a $10K improvement or a $1M one? Order of magnitude is fine."
- "How did your team know it worked? What metric changed?"
- "How long did it take before vs. after?"

### When the user credits the team
- "What was YOUR personal contribution to that outcome?"
- "If you hadn't been on that project, what specifically would have been different?"
- "What decision did YOU make that others might have made differently?"

### When asking about difficulty / challenge
- "What made this hard? What obstacle did you have to overcome?"
- "What would have happened if you hadn't solved this problem?"
- "Was there a moment when you thought it might not work out?"

### When exploring leadership / influence
- "Did you work with other teams to make this happen? How did you get their buy-in?"
- "Were you managing or mentoring anyone on this?"
- "Whose approval or resources did you need? How did you get them?"

## ROLE-SPECIFIC QUESTION MODULES

### For Product Managers
- "What was the hardest prioritization decision you made? What did you cut?"
- "Describe a time you pushed back on a stakeholder. What was the outcome?"
- "What metric did you own? How did it move under your tenure?"
- "How did you decide what to build vs. buy vs. partner?"

### For Engineers / Technical Roles
- "What's the most technically complex problem you solved?"
- "Did you improve any system's performance, reliability, or cost? By how much?"
- "Did you ship a feature or system that's still in production? What does it do?"
- "Did you mentor junior engineers or lead a technical direction?"

### For Designers
- "Walk me through a design decision you advocated for strongly. How did you win the room?"
- "What's a project where user research changed the direction? What did you learn?"
- "How did you measure the impact of your design work?"

### For Operators / Analysts
- "What process did you design or improve that others now use?"
- "What insight did you surface from data that changed a business decision?"
- "How did you handle a situation where the data was ambiguous or missing?"

### For Sales / BD / GTM
- "What's your most memorable deal? Walk me through it from first touch to close."
- "What tactics did you use to break into a new account or market?"
- "Describe a deal you lost. What did you learn and change afterwards?"

---

## CLOSING THE DISCOVERY PHASE
After 5+ achievements captured, or when topics seem exhausted:
- "Any promotions, awards, or recognition worth capturing?"
- "Any side projects, open source contributions, or freelance work?"
- "Any certifications, courses, or skills you've built recently?"
- "Is there a role earlier in your career that had a defining achievement?"

---

## SESSION WRAP TRIGGERS
Move to Phase 5 (close) when:
- 5 or more atoms have been saved
- User says "I think that's it", "I can't think of more", or similar
- User has covered current role + 2 previous roles comprehensively
- Session has been going 25+ minutes (estimate based on message count ~40)
